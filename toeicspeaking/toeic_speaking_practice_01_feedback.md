# 📝 TOEIC Speaking Set 01 — 채점 결과 & 오답노트 (v3, 문장 교정판)

> 오타/쉼표/소문자는 채점에서 제외합니다. 대신 **내가 쓴 문장을 그대로 가져와서, 의도한 내용은 유지한 채 자연스러운 문장으로 교정**했습니다. 단어 하나만 짚는 게 아니라 문장 전체를 어떻게 다시 표현하면 좋을지 확인하세요.

## Q3. Describe a picture

**내 원문:**
> This is picture taken in the restaurant, there are 3 people in the background and 2 people are on the right side in the background they are working on full time, and on the right side they are looking at the steps, some items on the table, and the other items on the display shelves, i think this pickture seems like normal picture

**교정 문장:**
> This picture was taken in a café. In the background, there are three people, and on the right side, there are two more people who seem to be sitting and chatting. They also seem to be looking at some items near the counter. There are some items on the table, and other items are arranged on the display shelves. Overall, this looks like a typical, relaxed café scene.

**무엇이 바뀌었는지:**
- `restaurant` → `café`: 사진 속 장소를 다시 확인하고 정확한 단어로 교체.
- `they are working on full time` → `sitting and chatting`: "정규직으로 일하다"라는 뜻이 되어버려서, 실제 사진 속 동작(앉아서 이야기하는 모습)에 맞는 표현으로 교체.
- `looking at the steps` → `looking at some items near the counter`: 사진에 없는 대상(steps)을 지우고, 확실히 있는 대상으로 교체.
- 마지막 문장 `seems like normal picture` → `looks like a typical, relaxed café scene`: 애매한 "normal" 대신 분위기를 구체적으로 요약하는 문장으로 교체.

## Q4. Describe a picture

**내 원문:**
> This is picture taken in the office, there are 2 poeople, one is man who is wearing white shirts and smiling with hands touching with the next woman, and the other is woman, who is smiling and receving the hands touch, some items such as paper notebook is on the table, I think they are discussing and they match some results.

**교정 문장:**
> This picture was taken in an office. There are two people: a man wearing a white shirt, smiling and giving a high five to the woman across from him, and a woman who is smiling as she gives him a high five back. There are some items on the table, such as documents and a laptop. I think they are celebrating a successful result from a project or discussion they worked on together.

**무엇이 바뀌었는지:**
- `smiling with hands touching with the next woman` / `receiving the hands touch` → `giving a high five ... gives him a high five back`: 두 문장에 걸쳐 풀어서 설명하던 동작을 정확한 표현 하나로 압축. 문장이 짧아지고 뜻도 더 명확해짐.
- `some items such as paper notebook is on the table` → `some items on the table, such as documents and a laptop`: 사진에 더 잘 맞는 사물(문서, 노트북)로 구체화.
- `they match some results` → `they are celebrating a successful result`: "성과를 맞추다"라는 뜻으로 쓰인 어색한 조합을, "성과를 축하하다"라는 실제로 쓰이는 표현으로 교체.

## Q5

**내 원문:**
> I think I use normally 5hours, I have to use gpt all day long

**교정 문장:**
> I think I normally use messaging apps and email for about five hours a day, and I also use ChatGPT throughout the day to help with my work communication.

**무엇이 바뀌었는지:**
- `I use normally 5hours` → `I normally use ... about five hours`: 빈도부사(normally)는 일반동사 앞에 위치.
- 질문이 "email/메신저 같은 커뮤니케이션 툴"을 물었는데 답이 GPT로 바로 넘어가서, `messaging apps and email`을 먼저 언급한 뒤 GPT를 "업무 커뮤니케이션을 돕는 보조 도구"로 자연스럽게 연결.

## Q6

**내 원문:**
> I think communicate by message better than direct because it makes some time to think

**교정 문장:**
> I think communicating through messages is better than talking directly, because it gives you time to think before you respond. For example, when I receive a message, I can check the details carefully before replying.

**무엇이 바뀌었는지:**
- `communicate by message better than direct` → `Communicating through messages is better than talking directly`: 비교 구문은 `동명사(-ing) + is better than + 동명사(-ing)` 형태로 맞춤.
- `it makes some time to think` → `it gives you time to think`: "시간을 만들다"는 `give`와 짝을 이루는 표현.
- 예시 문장(`For example, ...`)을 추가 — 원래 답변엔 이유만 있고 예시가 없었음.

## Q7

**내 원문:**
> I agree that make it to virtual meetings, I have many experience about that, when i had meeting in person, There were some mistakes which I didn't prepare and some forgottens, but in the virtual meeting, I had some time to prepare so I made some results more effectively and make it more sucessful,

**교정 문장:**
> I agree with switching to virtual meetings. I have a lot of experience with this. When I had meetings in person, I sometimes made mistakes because I didn't have enough time to prepare, and I forgot some points I wanted to mention. But in virtual meetings, I had more time to prepare beforehand, so I was able to get better results and make the meeting more successful. As for advice, I'd suggest that management send the agenda in advance so everyone has time to prepare before each virtual meeting.

**무엇이 바뀌었는지:**
- `I agree that make it to virtual meetings` → `I agree with switching to virtual meetings`: 동사 자리에 동사원형이 그대로 들어가 있던 구조를 정리.
- `many experience` → `a lot of experience`: experience(경험, 개념)는 셀 수 없는 명사라 many 대신 a lot of.
- `mistakes which I didn't prepare` / `some forgottens` → `mistakes because I didn't have enough time to prepare, and I forgot some points I wanted to mention`: 의도한 의미(준비 부족으로 인한 실수, 깜빡한 내용)를 명확한 문장으로 재구성.
- **마지막 문장(경영진 조언)을 새로 추가** — 원래 답변에는 이 부분이 아예 없었음. 질문이 두 가지(의견 + 조언)를 물었으므로 반드시 둘 다 답해야 함.

## Q8

**내 원문:**
> The future of digital marketing will be begin at 9 30, and Ms laura chen will be speech

**교정 문장:**
> The keynote speech, "The Future of Digital Marketing," will begin at 9:30, and it will be given by Ms. Laura Chen.

**무엇이 바뀌었는지:**
- `will be begin` → `will begin`: 조동사 뒤에는 동사원형 하나만.
- `Ms laura chen will be speech` → `it will be given by Ms. Laura Chen`: speech는 명사라서 동사 자리에 못 들어감. "발표 제목"과 "발표자"를 구분해서 문장을 재구성.

## Q9

**내 원문:**
> There is a Workshop about social media strategy It will held at Room B and It will start at 10 45 and end at 12

**교정 문장:**
> There is a workshop about social media strategy. It will be held in Room B, and it will run from 10:45 to 12:00.

**무엇이 바뀌었는지:**
- `It will held` → `It will be held`: 조동사 뒤 수동태에는 `be + p.p.` 형태 필요, be 누락.
- `start at 10 45 and end at 12` → `run from 10:45 to 12:00`: 시작/종료를 각각 말하는 대신 `run from A to B` 구문으로 한 번에 정리하면 더 자연스러움.

## Q10

**내 원문:**
> Oh unfortuneately there is one break, there is a workshop on the same time, you don't have any reasons, you have to miss it

**교정 문장:**
> Unfortunately, your call overlaps with part of the Social Media Strategy workshop, which runs from 10:45 to 12:00. Since your call ends at 11:15, you'll only miss the first thirty minutes of it. I'd suggest joining right after your call ends, or asking a colleague to share their notes from the part you missed.

**무엇이 바뀌었는지:**
- 이 문항은 표현보다 **내용(시간 계산) 자체가 틀렸던 경우**입니다. 통화는 10:30-11:15, 워크숍은 10:45-12:00이라 겹치는 시간은 앞 30분(10:45-11:15)뿐인데, 원문은 "무조건 놓친다"고 답함 → 정확한 겹침 구간을 계산해서 반영.
- `you don't have any reasons, you have to miss it` → 대안 제시 문장(`I'd suggest joining right after ... or asking a colleague ...`)으로 교체: 이 문제 유형은 "상황 설명 + 대안 제시"가 핵심이라 대안이 꼭 있어야 함.

## Q11

**내 원문:**
> In my opinion, i prefer the one which to choose their own working hours, I think all people have their own conditions, so they have the time to work effectively and uneffectively and some work is sometimes have own scedule, so if they have fixed time on schedule they have to schedule on their own, and if i can choose my own time, i will schedule my schedule more effecectively, because i know what i want and more effectively time,. so that is what i think so

**교정 문장:**
> In my opinion, I prefer the option of choosing your own working hours. I think all people have different conditions and different times when they work best, so a flexible schedule lets them work more effectively. Some jobs also require different schedules depending on the situation, so a fixed schedule doesn't always fit everyone's needs. If I could choose my own working hours, I would be able to manage my time more effectively, because I know when I work best and what I need to get done. That's why I think flexible working hours are more effective overall.

**무엇이 바뀌었는지:**
- `i prefer the one which to choose their own working hours` → `I prefer the option of choosing your own working hours`: 관계대명사 구조가 무너져 있던 부분을 `the option of + 동명사` 구조로 정리.
- `they have the time to work effectively and uneffectively` → `different times when they work best`: 원래 문장은 "효율적으로도 비효율적으로도 일한다"는 모순된 내용이었는데, 의도(사람마다 잘 맞는 시간대가 다르다)에 맞게 재구성.
- `some work is sometimes have own scedule` → `Some jobs also require different schedules depending on the situation`: 문법이 무너져 의미 파악이 안 되던 부분을, "직무에 따라 필요한 스케줄이 다르다"는 의도로 추정해 재구성.
- `if they have fixed time on schedule they have to schedule on their own` → 삭제하고 `a fixed schedule doesn't always fit everyone's needs`로 교체: "고정 스케줄인데 스스로 정한다"는 모순되는 문장이라, 전체 논지(유연근무 지지)와 방향이 맞는 문장으로 바꿈.
- 마지막 `so that is what i think so` → `That's why I think flexible working hours are more effective overall`: 결론을 앞의 이유들과 연결해서 다시 강조하는 문장으로 마무리.
